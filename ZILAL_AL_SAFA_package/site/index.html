The brief & the site

A well‑loved neighbourhood park that the heat locks shut for half the year

Al Safa Park 2 (plot 3570616) is a 15,000 m² (~3.7‑acre) community park, roughly 90 × 165 m, bordered by a Governmental Health Center, a Governmental Intermediate School, Juma Masjid and a shopping centre, with villa neighbourhoods and public parking across Road 36.58 — a genuinely mixed community of families, students, seniors, worshippers and people with mobility needs.

Its usage data tells the core story: visits peak sharply around 6 pm and collapse through the middle of the day, when surfaces are unshaded and thermal stress is high. The park is effectively unusable for ~6 hours a day in summer.

Our brief, from the Dubai Municipality Neighborhood Parks Manual: an accessible everyday space that reflects the local community, with strong walkability, destination playscapes, climate comfort and a living events calendar — refined through the DM Blueprint approach.

The idea

Five moves that turn a hot lawn into a year‑round oasis

ZILAL (Arabic for “shades / canopies”) organises the whole park around one native tree — the Ghaf, the UAE's national tree — and one goal: keep people comfortable, connected and outdoors from 05:00 to 23:00, all year.

1

The Cool Loop

A continuous, 100%‑shaded walking/running/cycling circuit under solar‑pergola canopies, its path geometry optimised by AI so it stays in shade at 3 pm in July.

2

The Majlis Grove

The social heart: clustered shaded majlis rooms under a grove of Ghaf & Sidr trees, tuned to the 6 pm peak — family gathering, picnics, quiet seating.

3

Adaptive Play & Sport

Existing courts retained & shaded, plus an all‑abilities, sensory playscape designed with the neighbouring health centre and school in mind.

4

The Sponge Garden

Xeriscape planting, bioswales and AC‑condensate + greywater reuse cut irrigation demand — while retaining & augmenting the park's existing mature trees into a living desert garden.

5

The Solar Canopy Spine

PV pergolas shade by day and power lighting, misting and the app by night — the park runs energy net‑positive.

+

Community & commerce

Distinctive gateway entrances (Identity), food/craft market kiosks, a dog run, prayer space, and a seasonal events calendar — hitting the Manual's commercial & events targets.

Unique on every side · Innovation & interactivity

Not just shaded — the smartest, most interactive park in Dubai

Most park entries stop at nice renders. ZILAL is designed to be genuinely unique on every side: rooted in Emirati identity, run by an AI brain, alive with interaction, regenerative in energy & water, and a future‑proof platform that gets better every year. Each feature below is benchmarked against real built parks (incl. Dubai Municipality's own Quranic Park) so every “first” holds up to the jury.

01

The Comfort Brain DUBAI‑FIRST

A live digital twin + AI reads heat, UTCI comfort, wind, air quality & footfall in real time and routes you to the coolest seat, lane or majlis right now — while auto‑tuning misting, irrigation & lighting and giving Dubai Municipality a KPI dashboard. No Dubai park operates a real‑time thermal‑comfort twin. This is our core AI story.

02

Kinetic Mashrabiya Canopy SIGNATURE

A heritage mashrabiya screen reimagined as a responsive shade (Al‑Bahar‑Towers logic brought to a park): petals open and close to track the sun over the Majlis Grove — iconic, photogenic, and unmistakably Emirati.

03

Net‑positive Solar Canopy

The shaded spine is a photovoltaic canopy that shades the Cool Loop by day and generates more energy than the park uses — powering lights, misting, the Comfort Brain, EV & e‑scooter charging, and exporting the surplus.

04

“Every Step Counts” kinetic floor INTERACTIVE

Footstep‑energy tiles (Pavegen‑type) under the existing sports court and a signature Cool‑Loop stretch: players light their own night game, runners’ steps light the path in Islamic‑geometry patterns, and every step is live data for the Comfort Brain. Energy is symbolic — the value is engagement & data.

05

AR Ghaf Heritage Grove

Point a phone at the Ghaf & Sidr trees to unlock augmented‑reality Emirati heritage, tree ecology and Islamic‑garden planting stories — extending Quranic Park’s educational idea into AR, right by the neighbouring schools.

06

Interactive Water & Light Play

Sensor‑activated splash & mist play cools kids by day (evaporative cooling that doubles as fun); after dark a responsive light‑scape in Islamic geometry turns the social core into an evening destination.

How you interact — from arrival to after dark

  • Arrive: a “Walk‑to‑Light” threshold ripples light under your feet; the app shows today’s coolest route.
  • Move: AR pacer on the Cool Loop; the kinetic stretch tallies the community’s steps on a live totem.
  • Play & sport: footstep‑powered games for kids & teens; the People‑Powered Court lights up at night.
  • Gather: programmable majlis “living rooms” reconfigure for daily use, Ramadan, markets & school events.
  • Learn: AR heritage trees + an open comfort/energy/water dashboard for civic pride and STEM visits.

Why it stays unique for years

ZILAL is a platform, not a prop. The Comfort Brain learns every season; new app features, AR stories and light shows ship in software with no rebuild. Native Ghaf‑led planting and net‑positive energy mean it looks and performs better in year 5 than year 1 — and its open data lets Dubai Municipality replicate the model city‑wide.

The differentiator

A documented, reproducible AI design pipeline

The challenge asks not for AI‑made pictures, but for AI woven through the whole life cycle, with human‑led final decisions. ZILAL goes one step further than most entrants: AI not only designs the park, it operates it — the sixth stage below.

01 · INGEST

Site intelligence

Satellite/GIS & QGIS, sun‑path & wind‑rose, Google popular‑times footfall, review‑sentiment NLP, and the supplied DWG survey.

02 · GENERATE

Layout search

Parametric & generative search (Grasshopper‑style + generative models) over path & canopy geometry to maximise shade, walkability & program mix.

03 · SIMULATE

Comfort & flow

UTCI thermal‑comfort & shade ray‑tracing (Ladybug/Honeybee‑type tools) per hour and season; pedestrian‑flow modelling.

04 · FORECAST

Experience model

ML models for dwell time, capacity & inclusivity coverage by hour, tuned to the Manual's targets.

05 · VISUALISE

Render & narrate

Diffusion‑model renders (day & night) + this data‑driven narrative and a living scorecard.

06 · OPERATE

The Comfort Brain

AI doesn't stop at the drawing: a live digital twin runs the built park — routing people to shade, auto‑tuning misting/irrigation/lighting and learning every season.

Every stage tests multiple alternatives and passes quantified trade‑offs to the designers — the AI Methodology Report documents the tools, prompts and decisions behind the final scheme.

Human‑led throughout: AI expands options and quantifies trade‑offs; designers make every final spatial decision — exactly the balance Dubai Municipality asked for.

Practical application of data analysis

The numbers behind the design

What the data changes

  • Zoning by heat & flow: the quiet Majlis Grove sits in the coolest core; active edges face the busy 6 pm arrival.
  • Shade where it matters: canopies placed on ray‑traced hot‑spots, not evenly — more comfort per dirham.
  • Right‑sized capacity: tuned to the Manual's 150–400 visitors / 10k m² and 1–3 hr visit targets.
  • Seasonality: planting & events calendars scheduled to the climate year.

All figures are modelled/illustrative estimates for concept communication, grounded in the DM Manual targets, climate norms and public site data — to be firmed up with site survey data.

Commercial & Service Facilities Plan (a required deliverable)

A food & craft market cluster and café/kiosks sit at the main entry plaza and along the Cool Loop, sized to the Manual's ~15% leasable target — placed to activate the park at the 6 pm peak and generate revenue that offsets operations, without intruding on the quiet green core.

Phased delivery

Phase 1 — Comfort first

Cool Loop, Majlis Grove core, entrances & universal access. Brings shade and daily usability fast.

Phase 2 — Play, sport & water

Adaptive play, upgraded shaded courts & fitness, the Sponge Garden, restrooms & kiosks.

Phase 3 — Energy, AI & interactive

Solar Canopy Spine, the AI Comfort Brain & sensor network, kinetic mashrabiya, footstep‑energy hero zones, AR grove and the interactive light‑scape.

Reuse = savings

Retains the existing pitch, court and mature trees — cutting demolition, cost and establishment time.

Self‑funding operations

Kiosks, café and events (leasable ~15%) generate revenue; xeriscape + solar cut running costs — supporting long‑term viability.

Low‑maintenance by design

Modular prefab shade & PV, durable materials and native planting keep lifecycle cost and upkeep low.

On the signature tech — costed honestly

The Comfort Brain and sensor network are low‑cost, high‑impact software+IoT. The premium item — footstep‑energy tiles — is deliberately limited to ~100–300 m² of hero zones (the sports court + one Cool‑Loop stretch), justified on engagement, data & safety lighting rather than kWh, and sized to sit inside the AED 35M budget while the solar canopy carries the real energy load.
