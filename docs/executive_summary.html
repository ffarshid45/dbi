Concept + Preliminary Design

ZILAL AL SAFA (Arabic ẓilāl — “shades / canopies”) reimagines Al Safa Park 2 as a park that works all day, all year. Today the park is well-loved but the desert heat effectively closes it for roughly six hours a day in summer: usage collapses at midday and peaks only around 6 pm. Our single design goal is to reclaim those lost hours — using artificial intelligence, woven through the entire design process, to place every path, canopy and tree where the data says it does the most good.

The idea — five moves

The park is organised around one native tree — the Ghaf, the UAE's national tree — and Emirati majlis culture, expressed through five connected moves:

  1. The Cool Loop — a continuous, fully-shaded ~1 km walking/running/cycling circuit under solar-pergola canopies, its geometry AI-optimised to stay in shade at 3 pm in July.
  2. The Majlis Grove — the social heart: clustered shaded majlis rooms under a grove of Ghaf & Sidr trees, placed in the coolest core and tuned to the 6 pm peak.
  3. Adaptive Play & Sport — the existing football pitch and basketball court retained and shaded, plus an all-abilities, sensory playscape designed with the neighbouring health centre and school in mind.
  4. The Sponge Garden — xeriscape planting, bioswales and AC-condensate + greywater reuse that cut irrigation demand while retaining and augmenting the park's mature trees.
  5. The Solar Canopy Spine — photovoltaic pergolas that shade by day and power lighting, misting and the park app by night, making the park energy net-positive.

Supporting these: four distinctive identity gateways (one addressing each neighbour), a food & craft market and café, prayer space, restrooms, a dog run, and a year-round events calendar. On top sits a stack of four benchmarked Dubai-first innovations — the AI Comfort Brain, a sun-tracking kinetic mashrabiya canopy, a net-positive solar canopy and an interactive “Every Step Counts” kinetic floor — making the park genuinely interactive and unique on every side (see over).

Programme, AI & feasibility

A complete, compliant programme

The design delivers every element of the required brief: a ~1 km jogging track, inclusive play, retained sport, community & picnic space, native landscape, shaded seating, restrooms, drinking fountains, café/kiosks, prayer space, waste & recycling, bicycle parking, accessible drop-off, four entrances, and a Commercial & Service Facilities plan sized to ~15% leasable area — placed to activate the park at the evening peak and to fund its operation.

Artificial intelligence — the differentiator

The brief does not ask for AI-made pictures; it asks for AI integrated across analysis, ideation, development and decision-making, with human creativity and judgement retained. ZILAL is built on a documented, reproducible six-stage pipeline — Ingest → Generate → Simulate → Forecast → Visualise → Operate — in which AI expands options and quantifies trade-offs at every step, and the design team makes every final spatial decision. Uniquely, the pipeline does not stop at the drawing: the sixth stage is a live Comfort Brain digital twin that operates the built park — routing visitors to real-time comfort and auto-tuning misting, irrigation and lighting. No Dubai park does this. The full method is set out in the accompanying AI Methodology Report.

Feasibility — buildable within AED 35 million

At roughly AED 2,300 / m² across the 15,000 m² site, ZILAL is scoped to the stated budget by reusing what works (the existing pitch, court and mature trees), building in three phases, and letting commerce fund operations. Kiosks, café and events generate revenue that offsets O&M, while xeriscape planting and on-site solar cut running costs.
